About

My name is Daniel Lee, and I am a student aspiring to be a programmer and developer. I am currently a sophomore attending the University of Michigan, and my major is computer science in the College of Engineering, along with a minor in mathematics. My interest fields include algorithms, artificial intelligence, and machine learning.

History

I was born in Bryan, TX on March 23, 1999, when my father was completing his PhD degree at Texas A&M University. I spent my early childhood mostly with my brother who is only a year younger than me. We would always be together and act as if we were close friends. My pursuit in academics began when I first competed in a regional mathematics contest during middle school, where I found the joy of solving problems and gaining trophies. Since then, my passion for problem-solving deepened as I learned more about math and computer science.
I played ice-hockey since 3rd grade, and it quickly became my favorite sports. Unfortunately, due to how much time commitment I would have to make, I had to quit in high school. Instead, I started to play golf and joined the school varsity team, where I performed well.
Currently, I am a computer science student at University of Michigan (still passionate about problem-solving and enjoying golf), preparing for my next stage of life.

Projects

Chess Robot with Visual Recognition

This is a yearlong research project that I conducted during my senior year of high school. Chess has been my favorite board game, and I was curious if I could make my own chess robot. I decided to develop an algorithm for detection of chess pieces, which is a feature that has yet to be improved in today's chess robots. My algorithm is based on a research study by Danner and Kafafy from Stanford, and I used OpenCV library in Python for computer vision tools.

Play Chess!

I am not a competitive chess player myself, so I created a pretty good chess AI instead! This AI implements negamax with alpha-beta pruning, and it uses board evaluations to decide moves. Play and see if you can beat my algorithm!
